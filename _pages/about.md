I am a Ph.D. student in the [UVR Lab](https://uvrlab.org/) at Korea Advanced Institute of Science & Technology (KAIST), advised by Prof. [Woontack Woo](https://www.linkedin.com/in/wtwoo/). 

My research focuses on the digitalization of human behavior and human-object/scene interactions. By digitizing human behavior, I aim to bridge the gap between physical and virtual worlds, enabling the seamless transition of human presence into VR/AR/MR (XR) environments and vice versa. My goal is to facilitate seamless interaction between real and virtual. Currently, I am conducting research on interpreting human intention to infer the complex relationships between individuals and their surrounding objects and spaces.

I am currently a Ph.D. student in Culture Technology at KAIST under the supervision of Prof. Woontack Woo, where I also earned my M.S. degree (2023) with a thesis titled "3D Hand Motion Estimation From a Single Motion Blurred RGB Image". Previously, I received my B.S. in Electrical, Information and Physics Engineering from Tohoku University (2021) after completing my undergraduate research on VR avatar body-ownership under the guidance of Prof. Satoshi Shioiri.
